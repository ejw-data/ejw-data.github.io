Content Development

I truly enjoy developing professional development and learning materials. To gain experience with audiences, I often utilized my access to K-12 students and teachers to test out my abilities to conceptualize meaningful, motivating content. With mastery of talking to younger audiences, I found that presenting to graduate students and faculty to be much more effective since my messaging was more concise and my adaptations were based on real-time feedback from the audience.

Meaningful learning is very important to me because in my technical background I often felt that engineers become accustomed to receiving and providing information in unstructured, ambiguous masses.

A large part of an engineers value to the employer is their ability to categorize the meaningful and arbitrary information and focus on the project critical parts. I think this is an incredible skill to develop but best if applied after a minimum level of technical mastery and communications, training, and teamwork competency.
